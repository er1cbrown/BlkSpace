- **Libraries & General Public:** Require systems that respect intellectual freedom while complying with legal standards.  
- **Activist Groups:** Risk censorship of advocacy content (e.g., LGBTQ+ resources, racial justice materials), disproportionately affecting minority communities.  

**Failure to Address CIPA:** The app’s lack of precision undermines CIPA’s intent by over-blocking lawful content, violating minors’ right to access age-appropriate information.

- **Discrimination:** Over-blocking disproportionately targets marginalized groups (e.g., filtering LGBTQ+ health sites as “inappropriate”).  
- **Educational Harm:** Schools and libraries may inadvertently restrict access to critical resources, such as sexual health education or historical content on civil rights.

- **Code 2.9 (Secure Design):** The system’s inability to prevent misuse (e.g., activists circumventing filters via VPNs) highlights poor security practices. Lack of rigorous testing and failure to patch vulnerabilities exacerbate risks.  
- **Code 3.7 (Public Infrastructure):** As a tool deployed in schools and libraries, Blocker Plus requires meticulous maintenance. Ignoring updates and stakeholder feedback demonstrates negligence toward public trust.

To address ethical and functional shortcomings, Blocker Plus should implement:
