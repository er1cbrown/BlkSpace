The short answer is **no, you cannot use a standard "printer cable" (USB-A to USB-B) to send video from your MacBook to the Dell UltraSharp U2311H monitor.**

While that cable is often included with printers, it serves an entirely different purpose on this monitor. Here is the explanation of why and the correct way to connect your devices.

### 🤔 Why the "Printer Cable" Won't Work for Video
The Dell U2311H is an older model (released around 2010) that separates its functions[citation:1][citation:5].

- **USB Ports are a Hub (Not Video Input):** The monitor has a USB upstream port (the square-ish "printer cable" port) and four USB downstream ports[citation:4][citation:7].
    - **The "Printer Cable" function:** If you connect a USB cable from your MacBook to the monitor's **USB-Upstream** port, it activates the **USB Hub**. This allows you to plug a mouse or keyboard into the monitor, which then sends *data* to your laptop[citation:8].
    - **What it does NOT do:** It does **not** send a video signal to the screen.

### 🔌 The Correct Way to Connect
Your MacBook is modern (USB-C), but the Dell U2311H uses older video ports. To get a picture, check the back of the monitor for one of these three inputs and use the corresponding adapter[citation:5][citation:10]:

| Monitor Port | Best For | Required Adapter for MacBook (USB-C) | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DisplayPort** | **Best Quality** | USB-C to **DisplayPort** cable or adapter | This is the most modern port on the monitor and is designed for computer video[citation:6]. |
| **DVI-D** | Good Quality | USB-C to **DVI** cable or adapter | DVI carries high-quality digital video (1080p), but no sound. |
| **VGA** | Last Resort | USB-C to **VGA** cable or adapter | This is analog. The picture quality will likely be noticeably "fuzzier" than the other options[citation:6]. |

### ⚡ A Note on the USB Ports
If you want to use the USB ports on the side of the monitor as a hub for a mouse or keyboard, you actually need **two cables** connected to your laptop:
1.  The **video cable** (DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA) for the screen.
2.  The **USB "printer" cable** (USB-C to USB-B, or USB-C to USB-A adapter + USB-A to USB-B) for data[citation:4].

### 💎 Summary
To get this working, look for a **USB-C to DisplayPort** adapter or cable. This will give you the cleanest 1080p picture from your MacBook to the Dell screen. The "printer cable" is only for extra USB ports.
