# First Run Security Guide

**Read this BEFORE you create your BlkSpace account.**

This takes 5 minutes. It will save you from losing your account forever.

---

## What Makes BlkSpace Different

**BlkSpace is NOT like Instagram or Twitter.**

- **No company owns your account** — you do
- **No "Forgot Password" button** — there's no password
- **No customer support** to recover your account
- **Your recovery phrase is everything** — lose it, lose your account

This is **freedom with responsibility.**

---

## The First Run Wizard

When you open BlkSpace for the first time, you'll see a **Welcome Wizard**. Here's what each step does:

### Step 1: Create Your Profile
- **Handle**: Your username (like @campus_king)
- **Display Name**: Your real name or whatever you want people to see
- **Town**: Your HBCU (TSU, Howard, FAMU, etc.)

### Step 2: Your Recovery Phrase

This is the **most important step.**

BlkSpace will show you **12 words** like this:

```
abandon ability able about above absent absorb abstract absurd abuse
```

**These 12 words = your entire account.**

### Step 3: Write It Down

**DO THIS NOW:**

1. **Get a pen and paper** (not your phone, not a screenshot)
2. **Write down all 12 words** in order
3. **Write down the numbers** (1-12) next to each word
4. **Store the paper somewhere safe**:
   - In a locked drawer
   - In your wallet
   - In your Bible
   - Somewhere only YOU can find it

**NEVER DO THIS:**
- Screenshot it
- Text it to yourself
- Save it in your notes app
- Email it to yourself
- Take a photo of it

If someone gets your 12 words, they **own your account forever.**

### Step 4: Verify Your Phrase

BlkSpace will ask you to **type 2 random words** from your phrase.

This proves you wrote it down.

**If you can't type them, you didn't write it down properly.** Go back and write it again.

## Why No "Forgot Password"?

BlkSpace uses **cryptographic keys** instead of passwords.

- Your **key** is stored on your computer
- Your **recovery phrase** can recreate your key anywhere
- **Nobody else** has your key — not BlkSpace, not GitHub, not anyone

This means:
- **Good**: No hacker can steal your account from a server
- **Good**: You can use your account on any device
- **Bad**: If you lose your phrase, nobody can help you

---

## Moving to a New Computer

If you get a new laptop or want to use BlkSpace on another device:

1. Install BlkSpace on the new computer
2. Click **"Recover Account"** (not "Create Account")
3. Type your **12-word recovery phrase**
4. Your account appears exactly as you left it

**Your WeixBucks, posts, followers — everything transfers.**

---

## What If Someone Steals My Phrase?

**Change your account immediately:**
1. Open BlkSpace
2. Go to Settings → Security
3. Click **"Generate New Key"**
4. Write down the NEW phrase
5. The old phrase becomes useless

**If someone already took your account** with your old phrase, you can't get it back. This is why you keep it secret.

---

## What If I Lose My Phrase?

**Before you lose it:**
1. Open BlkSpace
2. Go to Settings → Security
3. Click **"Show Recovery Phrase"**
4. Write it down again
5. Store it in a second safe place

**After you lose it and can't log in:**
- Your account is gone forever
- There's no reset
- There's no support ticket
- **Prevention is your only protection**

## Security Checklist

Before you finish setup:

- [ ] I wrote down my 12-word phrase on paper
- [ ] I stored the paper in a safe place
- [ ] I did NOT screenshot or photograph it
- [ ] I verified 2 words in the BlkSpace wizard
- [ ] I understand there is no "Forgot Password" option

## For Parents / Guardians

If you're setting up BlkSpace for a younger student:

1. **Write down the phrase yourself** and store it securely
2. **Explain the importance** — they can't just reset like other apps
3. **Consider** writing a second copy and storing it in a different location
4. **Never** store the phrase in a shared family cloud account

---

## For Testers on Multiple Devices

If you're testing BlkSpace on multiple computers:

1. Create account on **Device 1**
2. Write down the phrase
3. On **Device 2**, choose "Recover Account" and type the phrase
4. Both devices now share the same account
5. Any post on Device 1 appears on Device 2

**For testing:** You can create separate test accounts on each device. Just label them clearly (e.g., "test-tsu-laptop", "test-famu-desktop").

## Questions?

**"Can I change my handle?"**
Yes, in Settings. But your key stays the same.

**"What if my computer crashes?"**
Your account is fine — just recover with your phrase on a new computer.

**"Can I share my account with a friend?"**
Never share your phrase. If someone needs access, create a separate account for them.

**"Is my key sent to the internet?"**
No. Your key never leaves your computer. Only signed messages go out.

**"What if I forget which words are mine?"**
Open BlkSpace → Settings → Security → Show Recovery Phrase.
